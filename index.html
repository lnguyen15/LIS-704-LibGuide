Introduction

712 History of the Printed Book

This guide was created to support LIS 712: History of the Printed Book. This course covers the importance of spreading knowledge through printing and publishing from the implementation of the printing press in the 15th century to the present. Concepts to be explored will be how the invention of printing changed society, how the use of books has changed throughout history, and how to analyze books to determine its historical context.

In order to support this course, this guide seeks to provide students with reference sources to help with assignments or to better understand the course content. In addition to this, the guide will provide sources that look at the history of printing from many perspectives and from different eras.

      Web Resources

      Open Access Websites

      Image from kisscc0.com

       

      Early Modern Literary Studies

      This open access website is run by the Early Modern Literary Studies Journal. It is a journal dedicated to study of 16th and 17th century literature, focusing on English literature. The journal contains pertinent articles on print culture in literature.

      For LIS 712, this is a great web resource to explore. The website gives students full access to the journal's current and past issues and has a simple interface. There is a search function for students to further refine their research.

      Hand Papermaking

      Hand Papermaking is a site dedicated to the craft of making paper. This website offers photographs, portfolios, and free articles all about papermaking.

      This is a handy resource for LIS 712 in that it gives access to great visual resources on papermaking. Since the class focuses on identifying types of paper, this would be a good place to research papermaking for students.

      Libellarium: journal for the research of writing, books, and cultural heritage institutions

      Libellarium is an extensive open access journal that covers everything from the history of books, print, written word and more. The journal is published by the University of Zadar in Croatia.

      This journal's website is a rich resource that houses plenty of relevant articles for LIS 712. Students can read full-text PDFs of articles on print history, literacy, and publishing. They offer eBook compatible downloads for many of their articles as well.

      Language and Literacy

      This journal studies "literacy, learning, teaching, research, and theory." It is Canadian ran journal published by the University of Alberta.

      A literacy journal would be a good start for LIS 712 students in understanding how literacy plays into book publishing trends. The journal covers the history and development of literacy that spans multiple disciplines.

      Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ)

      The Directory of Open Access Journals is the project of Lund University in Sweden. DOAJ offers access to over 10,000 open access scholarly journals, allowing users to download full-text articles in disciplines including science and the humanities.

      This web resource is great for students because it functions as a search engine for many free access peer-reviewed journals. Not only would students find this useful for LIS 712, where they can find articles on the history of books and printing, but it would be a helpful resource for their other classes as well.

      The Achiever's Journal

      This journal is dedicated to studying English language, literature, and culture. It is published by Bhadohi Publishers in India.

      The Achiever's Journal is good for general research into English literature from an international perspective. It would be helpful for LIS 712 students to browse the issues for articles relating to the culture of English culture and books.

      Bibliotecae Journal

      Bibliothecae is run by the University of Bologna in Italy. The topics covered are information services, libraries, bibliographic information, and books according to their focus and scope statement.

      This online journal would help LIS 712 with finding resources about many topics relating to printing, including typography, book making, and book binding. The search function is easy to navigate and the scope of the journal is wide to assist students researching any aspect of book culture.

      Journal of European Periodical Studies

      This journal examines aspects of European periodicals from newspapers to advertisements and illustrations that appear in periodicals. The time frame covered is from the 17th century to the present.

      While this journal specifical covers periodicals and not books, it may still be a good source for LIS 712 students to look at the overall print culture that existed in Europe to better understand overarching historical contexts. This site allows students to search for articles that cover national and historical trends within Europe.
